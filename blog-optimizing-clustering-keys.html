Optimizing Snowflake Table Clustering Keys Automatically: First Results from Insights

By Goldilox Technology · April 29, 2025

Editor's note: This was our first published benchmark, from April 2025. We've since improved the models considerably — our current TPC-DS results show 51% faster queries and 76% fewer partitions scanned. See the latest benchmarks.

Snowflake's micro-partition architecture rewards good clustering: when a table is organized along the columns your queries filter on, Snowflake can prune away the partitions a query doesn't need and scan only what matters. Less data scanned means faster queries and lower compute cost. The hard part is choosing the right clustering key — and keeping it right as your workload changes.

Why manual tuning doesn't scale

Picking clustering keys by hand runs into three problems:

  • Workloads evolve. The key that was optimal last quarter can be wrong today as queries and dashboards change.
  • Visibility gaps. It's genuinely hard to know which columns your users filter on most across hundreds of tables and thousands of queries.
  • It takes expertise and time. Doing this well demands senior data engineers, and they have better things to do than re-tune clustering keys by hand.

What Insights does

Goldilox Insights automates the work. It continuously monitors your live query patterns, analyzes how each table is actually accessed, and recommends the clustering key that would maximize partition pruning — and it adapts those recommendations over time as your workload shifts. Every recommendation comes with an estimated saving so you can prioritize by impact.

The test

To measure the effect, we ran against a TPC-DS dataset at 10 TB scale: 24 tables, roughly 56 billion rows, and the full suite of 99 queries. The methodology was straightforward:

  1. Run the benchmark to establish a baseline.
  2. Let Insights analyze the workload and produce clustering key recommendations.
  3. Apply the recommended keys to eight high-impact tables.
  4. Re-run the benchmark and compare.

Results

With no manual tuning — just applying what Insights recommended — the workload improved across the board:

MetricBeforeAfter
Overall partition pruning41%59%
Total query execution time1,004 s870 s (−12%)
STORE_SALES pruning40%69%

The biggest single win came from STORE_SALES, one of the largest fact tables in the schema, where pruning jumped from 40% to 69%. That pattern — the largest, most-scanned tables seeing the biggest gains — is exactly what you want, because those tables are the ones that cost the most to scan.

A note on the benchmark

Our TPC-DS dataset is a copy we loaded ourselves, so it may differ from Snowflake's officially tuned benchmark environment. We use it because it simulates a realistic customer scenario: real tables, real query patterns, and clustering that starts from an unoptimized state — the situation Insights is designed to fix.

The takeaway

Clustering optimization doesn't have to be a manual, expert-only chore that decays the moment your workload changes. With systematic automation, you can keep tables tuned as your data environment evolves — and capture the compute savings continuously, not just once.
